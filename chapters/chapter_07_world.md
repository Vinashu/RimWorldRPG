## Factions

### Goodwill

**When it applies**
When tracking your relationship with a faction.

**How it works**
* Goodwill ranges from **-100 to +100**.
* Use the table below to interpret the relationship.

| Status | Goodwill | Effects |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ally** | +75 to +100 | Will send military aid. Better trade prices (**-10% Buy / +10% Sell**). |
| **Neutral** | -9 to +74 | Will trade. Will not attack unprovoked. |
| **Hostile** | -100 to -10 | Will attack on sight. Cannot trade. |

[Art: diagrams/ch07_goodwill_meter_01.png
Purpose: Visual goodwill meter for Ally/Neutral/Hostile bands]

**Edge cases**
* Trade prices use [Chapter 5: Equipment & Economy](chapter_05_equipment.md).

**Example: Goodwill**
A faction at **+80 Goodwill** is an **Ally**. They will trade and may send aid during raids.

### Major Factions

**When it applies**
When choosing nearby groups or encounter tables.

**How it works**
Use the faction list below to frame encounters and trading.

1. **Civil Outlander Union** (Tech: **Industrial 2**) — Survivors rebuilding society. Friendly. Source of Components and Medicine.
2. **Rough Pig Union** (Tech: **Industrial 2**) — Tough, xenophobic pig-people. Tense interactions.
3. **Savage Tribe** (Tech: **Neolithic 2**) — Aggressive warriors. Low tech, high numbers. Source of Pemmican/Jade.
4. **The Shattered Empire** (Tech: **Spacer 3**) — Honor-bound remnants of a stellar empire. High-tech trade (Title required).
5. **Pirate Gangs** (Tech: **Industrial 3**) — Always Hostile. Raiders who live to plunder.

[Art: spot/ch07_major_factions_01.png
Purpose: Add visual flavor for major faction types]

**Edge cases**
* Tech Levels and item requirements are in [Chapter 5: Equipment & Economy](chapter_05_equipment.md).

**Example: Major Factions**
The party meets a **Civil Outlander Union** caravan and trades for **Industrial Medicine**.

### Social Interactions

**When it applies**
When negotiating with factions or prisoners.

**How it works**
* **Trading**: Improve prices by **+2% per Social Skill Point**, max **±40%**.
* **Recruitment**: Break down a prisoner’s Will, then test Social to recruit.
* **Peace Talks**: Attend talks to raise Goodwill (**+50**) or worsen it (**-20**).

**Edge cases**
* Recruitment rules appear in [Chapter 6: Colony Management](chapter_06_colony.md).

**Example: Social Interaction**
Viper succeeds at Peace Talks and raises Goodwill by **+50** with a tribal faction.

## Biomes

### Temperate Forest

**When it applies**
When the colony starts in this biome.

**How it works**
* **Baseline**: No special penalties.
* **Disease**: Standard frequency.

**Example: Temperate Forest**
The colony experiences no farming penalties and standard disease risk.

---

### Arid Shrubland and Desert

**When it applies**
When the colony starts in hot, dry regions.

**How it works**
* **Heat**: Colonists without Dusters/Cowboy Hats gain **+1 Stress/Scene** during the day.
* **Scarce Food**: **Bane** on Foraging checks. **-2 Farming** (Base Modifier).
* **Visibility**: **+1 Base Defense**.

**Edge cases**
* Farming and Defense rules are in [Chapter 6: Colony Management](chapter_06_colony.md).

**Example: Desert**
Without Dusters, the colony gains **+1 Stress** during daytime work scenes.

[Art: spot/ch07_desert_gear_01.png
Purpose: Reinforce heat/gear requirement in desert biomes]

---

### Boreal Forest and Tundra

**When it applies**
When the colony starts in cold regions.

**How it works**
* **Cold**: Colonists without Parkas take **1d4 Damage** every hour.
* **Snow**: Deep snow counts as **Difficult Terrain** (Move costs +1 AP).
* **Winter**: **-1 Farming** (Base Modifier). **+1 Base Security**.

**Edge cases**
* Combat movement and AP costs are in [Chapter 4: Survival & Combat](chapter_04_combat_and_survival.md).

**Example: Tundra**
A colonist without a Parka takes **1d4 Damage** per hour until they find shelter.

---

### Tropical Rainforest

**When it applies**
When the colony starts in dense jungle regions.

**How it works**
* **Disease**: **Bane** on Vigor checks against disease.
* **Dense**: Movement is slow (Difficult Terrain default). **-1 Base Defense**.
* **Rich Soil**: **+1 Farming** (Base Modifier).

**Edge cases**
* Disease checks use the Skill system in [Chapter 3: Skills & Checks](chapter_03_skills_and_checks.md).

**Example: Rainforest**
The colony gains **+1 Farming** but suffers **-1 Base Defense** because of dense cover.

## Bestiary

### Stat Block Legend

**When it applies**
When the Storyteller uses a creature in play.

**How it works**
* **Attributes**: Vigor / Dexterity / Mind / Spirit.
* **HP**: `15 + (Vigor × 2)`.
* **Attack**: Weapon (Damage | Band | AP).
* **Skills**: Primary skill bonuses for Storyteller rolls.
* If AP is not listed, use the weapon’s AP from [Chapter 5: Equipment & Economy](chapter_05_equipment.md).

**Edge cases**
* Combat resolution is in [Chapter 4: Survival & Combat](chapter_04_combat_and_survival.md).

**Example: Stat Block**
A creature with **Vigor 3** has **HP 21** (15 + 3×2).

### Human Threats

**When it applies**
When the colony faces human raiders.

**How it works**
Use the stat blocks below. HP assumes average stats.

**1. Tribal Warrior** (Tech: Neolithic)
* **Stats**: 2 / 1 / 0 / 1. **HP**: 19. **Armor**: 0.
* **Skills**: Melee +1, Shooting +1.
* **Weapon**: Ikwa (4 | Melee) or Short Bow (3 | Close).
* **Tactic**: Zerg rush.

**2. Pirate Gunner** (Tech: Industrial)
* **Stats**: 2 / 2 / 1 / 1. **HP**: 19. **Armor**: 3 (Vest).
* **Skills**: Shooting +2.
* **Weapon**: Revolver (4 | Close) or Bolt-Action (6 | Far).
* **Tactic**: Uses cover, flanks.

**3. Elite Mercenary** (Tech: Spacer)
* **Stats**: 4 / 3 / 2 / 2. **HP**: 23. **Armor**: 6 (Marine).
* **Skills**: Shooting +4, Melee +3.
* **Weapon**: Charge Rifle (8 | Medium | 2).
* **Tactic**: Coordinated fire. Uses Stimpacks.

**Edge cases**
* Weapon stats are in [Chapter 5: Equipment & Economy](chapter_05_equipment.md).

**Example: Human Threat**
A **Pirate Gunner** shoots with **Shooting +2** and deals **4 Damage** on a Tier 2 hit.
